A founder told me last week that they needed help with their product.
We talked for an hour. By the end of the call we had not discussed a
single piece of software. We had discussed: who answers the support
inbox at night, what happens when a payment fails, how the onboarding
email gets written, who decides when to refund a customer, and whether
the people running the warehouse get notified before or after the
customer does.

None of that is product, in the way the word usually gets used. All of
it determines whether the product works.

## The misnaming

In the last fifteen years _product_ has eaten an enormous amount of
territory. It now includes things that used to be called operations,
support, marketing, and — in the bad cases — strategy. This is mostly a
naming problem, but naming problems have consequences. If you call a
service a product, you tend to staff it like a product, which means you
tend to under-staff the parts of it that are not software.

The interesting work, in most companies I see, is in the parts that
are not software. Not because software is unimportant, but because the
software is usually fine. The handoffs around it are not.

## What service design actually does

Service design, as a discipline, has a few unglamorous habits:

- It maps every step a person takes to get a thing done, including the
  steps that happen offline, in someone's head, or in a meeting.
- It pays attention to the people doing the work as well as the people
  receiving it. Internal teams are users.
- It cares about the lifecycle, not the launch — what happens at month
  six, month twelve, month thirty-six.
- It treats the operating model as a design problem, with the same
  rigor as the interface.

None of that is glamorous, none of it produces a screenshot you can
post, and most of it pays for itself within a quarter.

## The thing I want more founders to do

When you say the word _product_, stop and ask whether you mean it.
Often you mean: the whole thing — software and humans and policies and
the way the office runs. If so, design the whole thing. The interface
is downstream of all of it.
